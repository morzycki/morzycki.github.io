Marcin Morzycki

Students

Doctoral students whose committees I’m chairing or have chaired (two are named Ai, coincidentally):

  • Adam Gobeski. Dissertation in progress on temporal adverbials and factor phrases.
  • Cara Feldscher. Working (among other things) on interpretations of additive expressions like additional, which are ambiguous between degree and event-summing readings (e.g. Floyd traveled an additional six miles the next day).
  • Josh Herrin. Working (among other things) on superlatives, of, and fronting of degree words inside nominals (the tallest of people, so tall (of) a person).
  • Yan Cong. Working (among other things) on subjectivity in Mandarin.
  • Ai Taniguchi. Defended in 2017. Dissertation: The Formal Pragmatics of Non-At-Issue Intensification in English and Japanese. Now in a renewable semantics position at Carleton University in Ottawa. Not identical to Ai Kubota or Ai Matsui.
  • Curt Anderson. Defended in 2016. Dissertation on attenuation, intensification, approximation, and slack regulation. Currently a postdoc at the University of Düsseldorf.
  • Ai Kubota. Defended in 2016. Dissertation on evaluative adverbs and related issues in Japanese. Now a postdoc at the National Institute for Japanese Language and Linguistics (NINJAL). Formerly Ai Matsui. Not identical to Ai Taniguchi.
  • Olga Eremina. Defended in 2011. Dissertation on indefinites in Russian. Now at the National Research University Higher School of Economics in Moscow. Most recently teaching at Middlebury and Dartmouth Colleges.
  • Irina Agafonova. Defended in 2010. Dissertation on conjunction in Russian. Her work had a computational component, on which she was advised by John Hale. Most recently teaching at Concordia University.
  • Matt (Elvis) Husband. Defended in 2010. Dissertation on stativity and aspectual composition. Now an associate professor at the University of Oxford.
